INSTRUCTIONAL RESPONSIBILITIES

  1. Speaks with a modulated voice, clear diction, pronunciation and articulation.

  2. Shows effective non-verbal communication like facial expressions and gestures.

  3. Uses appropreate words in class.

  4. Demonstrates a deep and complete knowledge of theory and content in subjects of specialization appropriate to the year level.

  5. Keeps abreast with new developments in his/her area of expertise.

  6. Recognizes the relationship between his/her subject matter to other areas of descipline.

  7. Respons reasonably to students' descipline problems.

  8. Sets appropriate instructions and expectations for students clearly, and implements such instructions firmly.

  9. Provides a classroom set-up conductive for learning.

  10. Ensures the organization and availability of materials that will be used in class.

  11. Monitors students' punctuality and attendance.

  12. Maintains discipline inside the classroom.

  13. Productively utilizes the time allotted for class.

  14. Comes to class prepared and ready to teach.

  15. Implements instructional goals suitable for the students.

  16. Establishes a connection between the topics being discussed and the students' improvement, future profession, and life application.

  17. Uses and promotes technology-based resources in classrooms.

  18. Makes himself/herself available for questions and clarifications about the course.

  19. Uses other appropriate instructional materials aside from prescribed reference materials.

  20. Handles students with varying need and abilities by using appropreate approaches.

  21. Explains theories and concepts of the topic clearly and thoroughly.

  22. Asks logical, purposeful, and thought-provoking questions.

  23. Encourages students to participate in class by stimulating their ideas.

  24. Monitors and records students' performance in class.

  25. Shows effectiveness in facilitating quizzes and examinations.

  26. Returns corrected examinations, quizzes, exercises, case studies, researches, etc. to students on time.

  27. Gives appropriate feedback without embarrassing the students who commit mistakes.

  28. Provides intervention/remediation/consultation to students having difficulty in learning.
